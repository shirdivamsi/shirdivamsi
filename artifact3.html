Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning

Choosing the Right AI Approach Through Real-World Applications

Project Information

Course: Machine Learning Fundamentals

Artifact Type: Comparative Analysis of Machine Learning and Deep Learning

Tools Used: Academic Research, Technical Writing, Critical Analysis

Skills Demonstrated: Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Model Evaluation, Critical Thinking, Technical Communication

Project Highlights

  • Compared Machine Learning and Deep Learning using real-world applications.
  • Analyzed House Price Prediction using Linear Regression.
  • Analyzed Image Recognition using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs).
  • Evaluated the strengths and limitations of both approaches.
  • Learned how selecting the correct model depends on the type of data and business problem.

Project Overview

Artificial Intelligence offers multiple learning approaches, each designed to solve different kinds of problems. This project compares Traditional Machine Learning and Deep Learning by examining two practical applications: house price prediction using Linear Regression and image recognition using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). Through this comparison, I explored how the characteristics of data influence the selection of an appropriate AI model.

The project emphasizes that successful AI solutions depend not only on choosing powerful algorithms but also on understanding the problem, the available data, and the business objectives. This assignment strengthened my analytical thinking and improved my understanding of model selection in Artificial Intelligence.

Objective

The objective of this project was to compare Machine Learning and Deep Learning by analyzing their strengths, limitations, and real-world applications. The assignment helped me understand when each approach should be used and why selecting the appropriate model is a critical part of solving AI problems effectively.

Why I Selected This Artifact

I selected this artifact because it demonstrates my understanding of one of the most fundamental concepts in Artificial Intelligence—choosing the right learning approach for a given problem. While my previous artifacts focused on professional growth and the historical development of AI, this project highlights my ability to analyze different AI techniques, evaluate their practical applications, and communicate technical concepts in a clear and structured manner.

Case Study 1: House Price Prediction

Problem: Predicting residential property prices using structured numerical data.

AI Approach: Traditional Machine Learning

Algorithm: Linear Regression

Why This Approach: Linear Regression works well with structured datasets where relationships between variables are easy to interpret. It provides accurate predictions while maintaining transparency and explainability.

Real-World Example: Zillow's Zestimate uses machine learning models to estimate home values based on property characteristics.

Case Study 2: Image Recognition

Problem: Identifying objects and faces in digital images.

AI Approach: Deep Learning

Algorithm: Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

Why This Approach: CNNs automatically learn visual features such as edges, shapes, and patterns, making them highly effective for complex image recognition tasks.

Real-World Example: Apple Face ID uses CNN-based deep learning to securely authenticate users under different lighting conditions and facial expressions.

Key Learnings

  • Traditional Machine Learning and Deep Learning solve different types of AI problems.
  • Machine Learning performs well with structured data, while Deep Learning is better suited for complex unstructured data.
  • Selecting the appropriate algorithm depends on the characteristics of the data and the business objective.
  • Understanding the strengths and limitations of each approach leads to more effective AI solutions.
  • Real-world AI projects require balancing accuracy, interpretability, computational resources, and scalability.

Professional Reflection

Completing this project helped me realize that selecting the right AI approach is just as important as understanding the algorithms themselves. Before working on this assignment, I assumed that Deep Learning was always the better option because of its success in modern AI applications. Through this comparison, I learned that the most effective solution depends on the characteristics of the data and the specific problem being solved.

Comparing house price prediction with image recognition demonstrated how Traditional Machine Learning performs exceptionally well with structured numerical data, while Deep Learning excels when working with complex unstructured data such as images. This reinforced the importance of understanding business requirements before selecting an AI model.

As a Data Analyst pursuing graduate studies in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, this artifact strengthened my analytical thinking and improved my understanding of model selection. These concepts will help me design more effective AI solutions and make informed decisions when working with real-world datasets in future academic and professional projects.
